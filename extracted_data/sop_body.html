Key Definitions


Key Word

Definition

Wholesale Cost

Wholesale cost is the price a business (like a retailer) pays to buy products in large quantities directly from a manufacturer or distributor, significantly lower than the retail price to allow for bulk discounts and profit when reselling to the public. It covers the base cost of goods (materials, labor, overhead) plus a profit margin, enabling the business to mark it up and sell for a higher price to consumers.

Payment Voucher Status

This status shows where a suppliers payment request is in the payment disbursement process.

B2B (Business to Business) refers to any business orders that do not fall into the Platinum+ (or P+) category.

Confirmation of Charges

RPOps does not approve replacement part charges. Replacement parts are not covered in Damage & Defect allowances and should ship at the Supplier's expense.

The only exception is when replacement parts are ordered because part of a multi-box shipment is Lost in Transit. The Finance team has a process for handling these charges, so tickets should not be opened in these scenarios.  

Suppliers should not submit tickets to request charge approvals. If you receive a ticket for this, educate the Supplier of the correct process using the RP_Charges template.
