Hi [INSERT SUPPLIER NAME],

Thank you for reaching out. I'm happy to address this for you today.

Below is the difference between the lead time and the on-site delivery promise.

The Lead Time is set in the system by the supplier or by a Wayfair employee and is always counted in business days (5 days a week - Monday through Friday), while the Delivery Promise displayed on site is a customer-facing value expressed in calendar days (7 days a week - Monday through Sunday). This is why the Delivery Promise doesn’t equal the Lead Time in the system. 

Please see the below example to understand the difference between Lead Time to Delivery Promise:

480 hours Lead Time = 20 business days = 28 calendar days (weekends included)  = Delivery Promise of 4 weeks. 

Moreover, additional time pads are applied to prevent any unforeseen problem from delaying the delivery promise. Supplier Lead Time Pads are additional time pads applied on top of the base lead time (in 24h increments), used to adjust Lead Time per day of the week & are applied based on order date/day of week. Carrier Lead Time Pads  are cushions set to bridge the gap between base lead time and actual performance covering 95% of shipped orders. They are updated once every two weeks and cannot be set up on a SKU level.
